# Next Chapter: Hypotenuse

# How I got into Hypotenuse

After leaving Nanolumi, I knew my next move had to be different. I wanted to work at scale — not just thousands of users, but millions. I wanted to see how engineering orgs with real traffic make architectural decisions. And I wanted to work with AI in production, not just in research.

Hypotenuse was the perfect fit. An AI-powered ecommerce platform serving over a million users meant every deployment mattered. Every latency spike was a customer lost. Every downtime minute was real revenue. That's the kind of environment where you either level up fast or get left behind.

# What I've achieved

My first major impact was on reliability. The integration success rate for heavy-load operations was sitting at 89%. That's okay for a demo but unacceptable for production. I drove it to a 99.99% SLA — a difference that meant thousands of successful customer transactions that would have otherwise failed.

I helped the platform learn from itself, making every interaction smoother than the last. And I streamlined workflows so that the end-to-end user experience feels effortless — from the moment someone lands to the moment they leave satisfied.

# What I'm learning

Scale changes everything. At a startup, you can afford to rebuild something three times. At this scale, you design for correctness up front because rolling back affects millions.

I've also deepened my FastAPI and React/TypeScript skills. Working with a modern Python async stack in production has been eye-opening. The request throughput, the memory profile, the deployment ergonomics — it's a different league from Django.

And AWS. When you're managing infrastructure for high-concurrency workloads, you start thinking in terms of cost-per-request and cold-start latency, not just "does it work."

# The next chapter

I'm excited about where AI is going. Not just the models themselves, but the infrastructure around them — the feedback loops, the evaluation pipelines, the monitoring. That's where the real value lives.

Hypotenuse is giving me the opportunity to build at the intersection of AI and production engineering. This is where I want to be.

# End words

If you're reading this and thinking about your next move — optimize for learning velocity. Not title, not compensation. Find the environment where you'll be forced to grow the fastest. That's what Hypotenuse has been for me.
