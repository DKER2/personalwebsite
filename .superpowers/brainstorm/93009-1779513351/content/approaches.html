3 Approaches for Your AI Freelance Site

Positioning yourself as an AI Developer for hire — Custom Models, AI Integration, Automation

A

Client-Facing Landing Page (Recommended)

Transform homepage into a sales page. Hero: "AI Developer for Hire" with clear value prop. Add Services section (Custom Models, Integration, Automation). Featured case studies with results. Prominent "Book a Call" CTA. Keep Experience for credibility. New: Testimonials, tech stack, process.

Pros

  • Highest conversion for freelance leads
  • Clear client journey
  • Positions you as a business partner, not just a dev

Cons

  • More content to write
  • Needs case study content
B

Portfolio + Services Hybrid

Keep the portfolio structure but add a top "Available for Freelance" banner. Reframe Projects as AI case studies with business outcomes. Add a new /services route. Update hero animation to cycle AI-focused titles. Add "Hire Me" button in header.

Pros

  • Less structural change
  • Still great for recruiters browsing work
  • Easier to maintain

Cons

  • Less direct for client conversion
  • Services buried behind navigation
C

Minimal Polish & CTA

Quick wins: Update title from "Geek" to your name + "AI Developer". Change animated titles to AI-focused roles. Add a "Hire Me" contact section at bottom. Update footer. Add Open for Work badge. Keep everything else as-is.

Pros

  • Fastest to ship
  • Low risk
  • Minimal code changes

Cons

  • Doesn't stand out
  • Missed opportunity to showcase services
  • Weak client conversion

My recommendation: Option A — It's the most effective for actually getting hired. The extra work pays off because clients immediately understand what you do and how to hire you.
